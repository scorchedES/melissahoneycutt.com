Real estate AI use cases

Sample systems for real estate businesses.

These examples show the types of AI-supported systems I design for real estate agents, teams, and small brokerages. Each system is built around a real business problem — with human review points and professional judgment kept in place.

About these examples: these are sample systems based on common real estate business problems, not client results. They show how a system would be designed and where human review fits — so you can picture what working together actually looks like.

Sample system

AI Lead Follow-Up System for Real Estate Agents

Turn scattered leads into a daily follow-up system that tells the agent who needs attention, why they matter, and what to say next.

The problem

An agent gets leads from open houses, referrals, website forms, and social media. Follow-up depends on memory, inbox searches, and scattered CRM notes. Warm leads get treated the same as cold leads. There's no reliable way to know who needs attention first — and warm leads quietly go cold.

System designed

What the workflow does

A lead follow-up workflow that categorizes leads by type, source, timeline, and next step. It creates a daily lead priority list, drafts follow-up messages, summarizes key notes, and reminds the agent when a human call is needed.

What AI does

The repetitive work

  • Summarize lead notes
  • Draft personalized text and email follow-up
  • Categorize lead type and timeline
  • Identify the next best action
  • Create a daily priority brief
  • Turn open house notes into CRM-ready summaries
What stays human

The agent

  • Reviews all client-facing messages
  • Makes the call
  • Confirms motivation and timing
  • Handles agency, compensation, pricing, and representation
  • Decides whether the lead is worth continued follow-up
Approval workflow

How the system stays under control

  1. AI drafts the follow-up message or summary.
  2. The agent reviews and edits before anything goes to a client.
  3. The agent approves and sends from their own inbox or text app.
  4. The system logs the follow-up task or note.
  5. Anything involving agency, pricing, negotiation, or legal interpretation stays with the agent.
Expected outcome

A clearer daily follow-up process, faster response time, and a more organized view of which leads need attention. The system reduces blank-screen writing, makes follow-up more consistent, and keeps the agent in control of client-facing communication.

AI Listing Marketing System for Real Estate Agents

Turn one property intake into a complete listing marketing package — MLS, email, social media, video, and open house promotion.

The problem

Agents need listing descriptions, social posts, email copy, video scripts, open house promotion, and property highlights — but creating all of it manually takes too long. The listing goes live before the marketing catches up, and the launch window underperforms.

System designed

What the workflow does

A property intake form feeds one structured workflow that produces every listing marketing asset in coordinated voice and format — ready for the agent to review, edit, and approve before it goes live.

What AI does

The repetitive work

  • MLS-ready property descriptions
  • Social captions and post drafts
  • Email and SMS announcement copy
  • Video and reel script outlines
  • Open house promotion content
  • Property highlight sheets
What stays human

The agent

  • Verifies all property facts and details
  • Reviews fair housing and compliance language
  • Approves pricing and positioning
  • Edits voice and tone for the local market
  • Approves every asset before it goes live
Approval workflow

How the system stays under control

  1. The agent fills out one structured property intake.
  2. AI drafts every marketing asset from that single intake.
  3. The agent reviews and edits each asset.
  4. The agent approves what goes live and what doesn't.
  5. Compliance-sensitive language (fair housing, fact claims) gets reviewed before publish.
Expected outcome

Listings launch with a complete, coordinated marketing package — without spending a day building it from scratch. The agent keeps full editorial control while reclaiming the hours that used to disappear into copywriting.

AI Buyer Consultation & Client Communication System

Turn buyer intake notes, showing feedback, and process questions into a clearer client communication system that helps the agent show value — before and after the buyer agreement.

The problem

Buyer agency conversations are harder than they used to be. Agents need to explain their value earlier, prepare stronger consultations, and communicate clearly throughout the buyer journey — but the prep work is inconsistent and the communication tools are scattered.

System designed

What the workflow does

A buyer-side workflow that prepares the consultation, structures the conversation, and feeds the post-consultation communication — so every buyer sees consistent value from intake through close.

What AI does

The repetitive work

  • Consultation agendas built from buyer intake
  • Buyer education materials and explainers
  • Property comparison summaries
  • Showing recaps and feedback summaries
  • Follow-up message drafts after each step
  • Timeline and process overviews
What stays human

The agent

  • Handles representation and agency conversations
  • Discusses compensation
  • Gives pricing and offer strategy advice
  • Runs the actual consultation conversation
  • Approves every client-facing message before sending
Approval workflow

How the system stays under control

  1. The agent fills out a buyer intake or imports notes.
  2. AI drafts the consultation agenda, education materials, and follow-up.
  3. The agent reviews and edits before the consultation.
  4. The agent runs the conversation — agency, compensation, and strategy stay with the agent.
  5. Follow-up communication is drafted by AI and approved by the agent before sending.
Expected outcome

Stronger consultations, more consistent client communication, and a clearer way to show value early in the buyer relationship — without the agent having to rebuild materials for every new buyer.
